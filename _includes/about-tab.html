Hi there! I'm Michael, an affiliated lecturer and postdoctoral research associate at the University of Cambridge. I am also a research associated at Fitzwilliam College, Cambridge. I work with Andreas Vlachos on automated fact verification at the Department of Computer Science and Technology. I graduated in 2021 with my PhD thesis from the University of Amsterdam, where I worked with Ivan Titov on building NLP models that incorporate structured data.

My research is focused on knowledge-intensive NLP, especially for fact-checking and problems with similarly complex epistemology. I am also very interested in modeling structured data sources, such as knowledge graphs, tables, or parse trees. I study technologies that improve the way we interact with information, whether that is through question answering systems that allow us to interrogate data, or fact-checking systems that help us reason about the evidential support for data.

I have a background in computer science, software engineering, with a sprinkle of cognitive science thrown in for good measure. I have an M.Sc. degree in It & Cognition from the University of Copenhagen and a B.Sc. degree in Software Engineering from the Technical University of Denmark.
