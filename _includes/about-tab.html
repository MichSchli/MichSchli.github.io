I am a lecturer at Queen Mary University of London, and an affiliated lecturer at the University of Cambridge. I am also a research associate at Fitzwilliam College, Cambridge.

My research is focused on automated reasoning over retrieved evidence, especially for fact-checking and problems with similarly complex epistemology. I am also very interested in modeling structured data sources, such as knowledge graphs, tables, or parse trees. I study technologies that improve the way we interact with information, whether that is through question answering systems that allow us to interrogate data, or fact-checking systems that help us reason about the evidential support for data.

Prior to this, was a postdoctoral research associate at the University of Cambridge, where I worked with Andreas Vlachos on automated fact verification. I graduated in 2021 with my PhD thesis from the University of Amsterdam, where I worked with Ivan Titov on building NLP models that incorporate structured data.
